Step 4

Methods of Examination

I
Inspection
Visual examination of body parts for color, size, shape, symmetry, movement
P
Palpation
Using hands to feel texture, temperature, moisture, organ size/location, tenderness
A
Auscultation
Listening to body sounds (heart, lungs, bowel) with stethoscope
P
Percussion
Tapping body parts to produce sound waves; assesses organ density, borders, fluid
M
Measurement
Recording height, weight, vital signs, circumference, range of motion
M
Mensuration
Measuring wounds, lesions, body parts; using tape measure, ruler

Step 6

Proper Body Mechanics

Essential rules to prevent injury when assisting patients and moving equipment:

Wide Base of Support
Keep feet shoulder-width apart for stability
Bend at Knees
Never bend at waist; use leg muscles to lift
Keep Back Straight
Maintain natural spinal curvature
Lift Close to Body
Hold objects close to center of gravity
Avoid Twisting
Pivot feet instead of twisting torso
Get Help for Heavy Loads
Use assistance or lifting devices when needed
