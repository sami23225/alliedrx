Safety & Emergency Practices in Action

Use this page to apply what you learned in Chapter 12 – Safety and Emergency Practices. Walk through common medical office scenarios and check your readiness to prevent hazards and respond to emergencies.

Activity 1

Quick Safety Walk-Through

Imagine you are opening the clinic for the day. Select each item that represents a safety hazard that must be corrected before patients arrive.

Activity 2

Match the Emergency to the First Response

For each situation, choose the best first action for the medical assistant. Think about office protocols, scope of practice, and the need to activate EMS.

Scenario A

A patient suddenly clutches their chest, becomes pale and sweaty, and says, “I think I’m having a heart attack.”

Scenario B

A child in the waiting room begins coughing after eating a snack. They are breathing but struggling and cannot speak in full sentences.

Scenario C

A patient calls the office reporting new-onset slurred speech and one-sided weakness that started 20 minutes ago.

Scenario D

You notice smoke coming from a wastebasket in an exam room.

My Emergency Readiness Plan

Reflection prompts

  • Which office hazards from Activity 1 are most likely in your current or future workplace?
  • How will you keep emergency equipment (crash cart, oxygen, AED) ready for use on every shift?
  • What steps will you take to stay calm and follow protocol when a real emergency happens?

Before you continue

Ready to Move On?

Before you mark this activity complete, make sure you have:

  • Selected the hazards in the Safety Walk-Through and reviewed the feedback.
  • Matched each emergency scenario with an appropriate first response.
  • Written at least one concrete step in your Emergency Readiness Plan.
  • Checked off the Telephone Triage & Documentation items you feel confident about.
