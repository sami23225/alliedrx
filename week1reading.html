How to work through this reading

  • □ Skim each chapter’s headings, subheadings, and summary before you start reading in detail.
  • □ As you read, highlight or note any key terms that are unfamiliar so you can look them up.
  • □ Pause after each major section and jot down 1–2 sentences about what you learned.
  • □ Complete any “Review Questions” or “Check Your Understanding” sections in the textbook.

Once you have finished Chapter 1 and Chapter 12, you can mark this unit as complete and move on to the next activity in the course.

Why this matters

How this reading supports your success

Chapter 1 helps you understand how this program is designed: you’ll be evaluated on specific competencies, not just test scores. Knowing this early will help you track your progress and focus your study time.

Chapter 12 shows how those competencies connect to real-world safety and emergency practices in the clinic. The habits you build here directly impact patient safety in every setting.

After completing this reading, you should be able to:

  • Describe what competency-based education means for you as a medical assistant student.
  • Identify your role in maintaining a safe environment and responding to basic emergencies.
  • Recognize where these concepts will show up again in skills check-offs, simulations, and exams.
Pro tip: Flag any sections on emergency procedures, safety protocols, and competency lists—these are high-yield topics for both skills validation and certification exam prep.
