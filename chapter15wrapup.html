Joan Rothman's Gastroenterology Practice

Case Scenario

Joan Rothman, CMA (AAMA), works for Dr. Raj Sahani, a gastroenterologist. Dr. Sahani has asked Joan to develop educational packets for common GI diagnostic studies and help orient new staff members.

Patient Education Packets Needed:

1. Colonoscopy Preparation: Patient needs clear instructions for bowel prep, diet restrictions, and medication management.

2. Fecal Specimen Collection: Instructions for collecting stool samples for occult blood, O&P, or C. diff testing.

3. Upper Endoscopy (EGD): Pre-procedure fasting instructions and post-procedure care.

Step 5

Chapter 15 Vocabulary

Dysphagia
Difficulty swallowing; common in esophageal disorders
Ascites
Abnormal fluid accumulation in peritoneal cavity; sign of liver disease
Jaundice
Yellowing of skin/mucous membranes from bile pigment deposition
Hepatomegaly
Abnormal liver enlargement; palpated in RUQ
Fistula
Abnormal connection between organs or to body surface
Anastomosis
Surgical connection between two structures (e.g., bowel segments)
Polyps
Mucosal outgrowths in colon; considered precancerous
Valsalva Maneuver
Straining against closed airway; increases abdominal pressure
