Step 2

Key Vocabulary Flashcards

Select each card to reveal the definition. Then try to explain each term in your own words—as if you were teaching a new team member in the office.

Holistic Care
An approach to healthcare that considers the whole person—their physical, emotional, social, spiritual, and cultural needs—rather than just focusing on specific symptoms or diseases.
SOAP Documentation
A structured method of documentation: Subjective (patient's reported symptoms), Objective (measurable findings), Assessment (diagnosis/clinical judgment), Plan (treatment and follow-up).
Therapeutic Communication
Purposeful, goal-directed communication that builds trust, reduces anxiety, and encourages patients to express feelings and concerns. Uses techniques like active listening, empathy, and open-ended questions.
Sign vs. Symptom
Sign: Objective, observable, measurable finding (fever, rash, blood pressure reading). Symptom: Subjective experience reported by the patient (pain, dizziness, fatigue).
Defense Mechanisms
Unconscious psychological strategies patients use to cope with anxiety, stress, or threatening situations. Common examples include denial, projection, rationalization, and displacement.
Meaningful Use (EHR)
Criteria established by CMS for using electronic health records effectively to improve quality, safety, efficiency, and reduce health disparities while maintaining privacy and security of patient information.

Supporting Chris & Improving Your Assessment Skills

Scenario

Chris Isaacson, an MA working in an ambulatory care clinic, is having difficulty gathering comprehensive information from patients. They don't always respond openly and honestly to him, and the attending physician isn't satisfied with his work. His supervisor needs to help him improve his interviewing skills.

Reflection Prompts

  • How can Chris develop helping relationships so that patients' medical histories are as comprehensive as possible?
  • Would displaying greater sensitivity to diverse populations help Chris gather more accurate information?
  • How can using active listening techniques and attending to patients' nonverbal behaviors help Chris develop therapeutic communication skills?
  • How can Chris's supervisor help him become a better communicator and demonstrate comprehensive and accurate documentation?
