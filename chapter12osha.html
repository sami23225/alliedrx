OSHA Hazard Finder

Use this activity to practice spotting and classifying OSHA-related hazards in a medical office.

Activity 1

Classify That Hazard

Choose the best OSHA hazard category.

  • 1. Overfilled sharps container with needles protruding.

  • 2. Unlabeled disinfectant bottle stored next to drink cups.

  • 3. ECG machine cords stretched across dim hallway.

  • 4. MA twisting to reach heavy charts overhead.

  • 5. Emergency exit blocked by stacked boxes.

Activity 2

Where Does This Hazard Live?

Match each hazard to the clinic zone.

Hazard A

Open blood collection tubes left on counter.

Hazard B

Toys & magazines scattered across floor.

Hazard C

Mop bucket left filled with water in hallway.

Hazard D

Oxygen tank not secured to wall or cart.

Apply it

Fix the Hazard – Your Plan

Reflection prompts

  • Which two hazards are most likely in your workplace?
  • Which OSHA standard applies to each?
  • Who must you communicate with to fix it?
Show an example hazard fix
Example: “During our morning huddle I will remind staff that sharps containers must be closed and replaced at the fill line. I’ll check each exam room weekly and document replacements.”

Skill focus

My OSHA Safety Checklist

  • I can name three OSHA hazard categories.
  • I know where SDS sheets & exposure plans are stored.
  • I understand why hazards must always be reported.
  • I can explain PPE & sharps disposal requirements.

Before you continue

Ready to Move On?

  • Wrote at least one hazard correction step.
  • Completed your OSHA checklist.
