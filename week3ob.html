Clinical Safety & Skin Assessment

Infection Control ‚Ä¢ Dermatology

Week 3 bridges infection prevention science with dermatological clinical skills. You'll learn how to protect both patients and healthcare workers from pathogens while developing expertise in skin assessment‚Äîthe body's first line of defense.

These chapters are connected because dermatology procedures (biopsies, wound care, lesion removal) require meticulous infection control. Every skin assessment begins with hand hygiene, and every procedure ends with proper instrument sterilization.

By the end of this week, you will be able to:

  • Perform proper hand hygiene and don/doff PPE according to Standard Precautions.
  • Document and describe skin lesions using correct dermatological terminology.
  • Implement OSHA bloodborne pathogen protocols in clinical scenarios.
  • Assist with dermatologic procedures while maintaining sterile technique.
  • Educate patients about infection prevention and skin cancer warning signs.
Clinical Insight: The ABCDE Rule for melanoma detection: Asymmetry, Border irregularity, Color variation, Diameter >6mm, Evolving. Early detection improves melanoma survival rates to over 99%!
Infection Control Tip: Alcohol-based hand rubs kill most pathogens in 15-20 seconds, but they don't remove visible soil or C. difficile spores. Always wash with soap and water when hands are visibly soiled or after caring for patients with diarrhea.
