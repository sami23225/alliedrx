Clinical Scenario: Pediatric ENT

Scenario

A 3-year-old presents with fever, ear pain, and pulling at left ear for 2 days. Mother reports recent cold symptoms. Otoscopy shows bulging, red tympanic membrane.

Assessment & Management

  • What is the likely diagnosis?
  • What type of hearing loss might this cause?
  • How would you position the child for otoscopy?
  • What patient education would you provide?

Diagnosis & Management:

Acute Otitis Media (AOM)
Conductive hearing loss
Treat: Antibiotics, analgesics, follow-up
Documentation Example:
"3yo with 2-day history of fever, ear pain, ear pulling. Recent URI symptoms. Left TM: bulging, erythematous, light reflex absent. Right TM: normal. Diagnosis: Acute otitis media left ear. Educated parent on antibiotic course, pain management, follow-up in 2 weeks."
Red Flags for Referral:
• Symptoms >48 hours on antibiotics
• Facial weakness (Bell's palsy)
• Meningeal signs
• Hearing loss persisting >3 months
