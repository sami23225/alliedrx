Clinical Connection

Why Documentation & Specialties Matter Together

Chapter 2 teaches you how to document—Chapter 13 shows you what to document. Every eye exam, otoscopic finding, and visual acuity test requires precise, legally sound recording.

In ophthalmology and otolaryngology practices, documentation errors can lead to incorrect prescriptions, missed diagnoses, or treatment delays. Your accuracy protects patient vision, hearing, and overall health.

After completing this reading, you should be able to:

  • Document a patient encounter using proper SOAP note format for eye or ear exams.
  • Identify common ophthalmology and otolaryngology equipment and their purposes.
  • Apply HIPAA guidelines when documenting sensitive visual or auditory health information.
  • Recognize when documentation requires specialist referral notations.
Clinical Insight: When documenting visual acuity, always record both uncorrected and corrected vision. For otoscopic exams, note ear canal color, cerumen amount, and tympanic membrane appearance. These details matter for tracking changes over time.
