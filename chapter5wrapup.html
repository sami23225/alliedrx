Step 2

Key Vocabulary Flashcards

Select each card to reveal the definition. Try to explain each term in your own words as if you were teaching a peer.

Holistic Model
An approach to patient education that considers emotional, social, spiritual, and physical needs—not just the medical condition.
Stages of Grief
Denial, anger, bargaining, depression, acceptance—emotional responses patients may experience when facing chronic illness or bad news.
Health Literacy
A patient's ability to understand and use health information to make appropriate decisions about their care.
Patient Empowerment
Providing patients with knowledge, skills, and confidence to actively participate in managing their own health.
Telephone Triage
Assessing patient symptoms and urgency over the phone to determine the appropriate level and timing of care.
Teaching Plan
A structured outline of educational objectives, methods, materials, and evaluation tailored to a specific patient's learning needs.

Apply it

Coaching Sam Ignatio

Case Study

Sam Ignatio, 62, was just diagnosed with Type 2 Diabetes. He has limited knowledge of the disease, is 50 lbs overweight, has hearing loss, and shows early signs of diabetic vision changes. His diet is high in sugar and saturated fat.

Reflection Prompts

  • How would you assess Sam's readiness to learn given his hearing and vision challenges?
  • What teaching methods would work best for him (e.g., visual aids, hands-on, family involvement)?
  • How can you empower Sam to take ownership of his diabetes management?
