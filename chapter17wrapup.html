Step 4

Stages of Pregnancy

First Trimester (Weeks 1-12)
• Major organ development • Morning sickness • Confirm pregnancy • Initial prenatal visit
Second Trimester (Weeks 13-27)
• Quickening (first fetal movements) • Anatomy ultrasound • Glucose screening • Growing belly
Third Trimester (Weeks 28-40)
• Rapid fetal growth • Braxton Hicks contractions • Fetal position assessment • Birth plan discussion
Labor & Delivery
• Early labor: contractions begin • Active labor: cervix dilates 4-7cm • Transition: 8-10cm dilation • Delivery: baby birth • Placental delivery

Step 5

Breast Self-Examination (BSE)

Teach patients to perform BSE monthly, 7-10 days after menstrual period starts.

1
Visual Inspection
Stand before mirror with arms at sides, then raised overhead. Look for changes in size, shape, contour, or skin changes.
2
Palpation Lying Down
Lie down with right arm behind head. Use left hand to examine right breast with pads of fingers in circular motion.
3
Vertical Strip Pattern
Use vertical strips covering entire breast from collarbone to bra line, and from armpit to breastbone.
4
Nipple Check
Gently squeeze nipple looking for discharge. Repeat steps 2-4 on left breast.
5
Report Findings
Report any lumps, thickening, dimpling, redness, swelling, or nipple changes to provider immediately.

Step 6

OB/GYN Diagnostic Tests

Key diagnostic procedures in women's health:

Pap Test
Screens for cervical cancer; collect cells from cervix. Start at age 21.
Colposcopy
Microscopic exam of cervix after abnormal Pap; may include biopsy.
Ultrasound
Imaging for pregnancy dating, fetal anatomy, pelvic masses.
Mammogram
Breast cancer screening X-ray; baseline at 40, annual at 45.
HSG
Hysterosalpingogram; evaluates uterine shape and fallopian tube patency.
LEEP
Loop electrosurgical excision procedure; removes abnormal cervical tissue.
