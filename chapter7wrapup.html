Step 2

Normal Vital Signs Ranges

Click each vital sign to review normal ranges and factors affecting measurements.

Temperature
Normal: 97.8-99.1°F (36.5-37.3°C)
Pulse
Adult: 60-100 bpm; Infant: 120-160 bpm
Respiration
Adult: 12-20 rpm; Infant: 30-60 rpm
Blood Pressure
Normal: <120/<80 mmHg

Blood Pressure Classification

Review the current ACC/AHA hypertension guidelines and recommended actions.

Recheck in 1-2 years with lifestyle education
E
Elevated: 120-129/<80 mmHg
Lifestyle modifications, recheck in 3-6 months
1
Stage 1: 130-139/80-89 mmHg
Lifestyle changes + consider medication if CVD risk
2
Stage 2: ≥140/≥90 mmHg
Lifestyle changes + antihypertensive medication
!
Hypertensive Crisis: >180/>120 mmHg
Immediate medical attention required
Korotkoff Sounds
Sounds heard during BP measurement: Phase I (systolic) to Phase V (diastolic)
Auscultatory Gap
Temporary disappearance of sounds between systolic and diastolic readings

Pro Tip from Carlos Ricci:

"Always allow 5 minutes of rest before taking BP. If the first reading is high, wait 2 minutes and retake. Document both readings and which arm was used. Never tell patients their pulse or respiration rate while counting – it changes the result!"
