What we're covering this week

Clinical Documentation & Specialty Care

Week 2 connects precise documentation with specialized clinical skills. You'll learn how accurate health records support patient care in ophthalmology (eye care) and otolaryngology (ear, nose, and throat care), where detailed records are essential for diagnosis and treatment.

These chapters work together because every clinical finding in eye and ENT exams requires meticulous documentation. From Snellen chart results to otoscopic observations, your recording accuracy directly impacts patient safety and treatment outcomes.

By the end of this week, you will be able to:

  • Document patient encounters using proper SOAP note format for ophthalmology and otolaryngology exams.
  • Prepare patients and assist with basic eye and ear examination procedures.
  • Maintain HIPAA compliance while managing sensitive visual and auditory health information.
  • Identify appropriate documentation for referrals to ophthalmologists or otolaryngologists.
Clinical Insight: In ophthalmology documentation, "OD" means right eye (oculus dexter), "OS" means left eye (oculus sinister), and "OU" means both eyes (oculi uterque). Confusing these abbreviations can lead to serious medication errors!
