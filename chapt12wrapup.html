Step 2

Key Vocabulary Flashcards

Select each card to reveal the definition. Then try to explain each term in your own words—as if you were teaching a new team member in the office.

Emergency Preparedness Plan
A written plan that outlines how the practice will prevent, respond to, and recover from emergencies such as fires, natural disasters, violent incidents, or community-wide events.
Automated External Defibrillator (AED)
A computerized device that analyzes a patient’s heart rhythm and, when needed, prompts rescuers to deliver a shock to treat life-threatening arrhythmias such as ventricular fibrillation.
Cerebrovascular Accident (CVA)
Also called a stroke; an interruption of blood flow to part of the brain that can cause neurologic damage, weakness, speech problems, or loss of consciousness.
Insulin Shock vs. Diabetic Coma
Insulin shock results from severe hypoglycemia and has a rapid onset; diabetic coma results from severe hyperglycemia and develops more slowly when glucose remains too high.
Good Samaritan Laws
State laws designed to protect individuals who provide emergency care in good faith at the scene of an emergency from being sued for ordinary negligence.
Standard Precautions
Infection control practices—such as hand hygiene, PPE, and safe sharps handling—used with all patients to reduce risk of exposure to blood and body fluids, especially during emergencies.
